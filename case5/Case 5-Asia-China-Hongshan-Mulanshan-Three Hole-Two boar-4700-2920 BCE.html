Hongshan-jade-three-hole, double-boar device-with base tenon-4700-2920 BCE

Case no.: 5

Accession Number:

Formal Label: Hongshan-jade-three-hole, double-boar device-with tenon-4700-2920 BCE

Display Description:

This porcine device has three tori (donut-shapes) with a domesticated boars head (note smiling face) at both ends. The three curves on the top surface of this artifact are thought to signify a mountain ridge, Mulanshan-where wild boars were sought. The undersurface of this artifact is curved to fit a dowel for mounting.
