Hongshan culture (紅山文化; pinyin: Hóngshān wénhuà) is a Neolithic culture in the Liao river basin from Inner Mongolia to Liaoning, and dated to 4700-2900 BCE. This Hongshan jade cong has two different masks, the top one depicting a deceased ancestor and the bottom one depicting the protective dragon. While it is diminutive in size this feature distinguishes it as being significant.
