Display Description:

This end pitted anvil was used in conjunction with a hammerstone to access the pithy fruit of seeds or nuts or marrow from small bones (see Leakey 1971, fig. 104, no. 12). Olduwan tools represent the earliest stone tool industry in prehistory. This artifact is from the Olduvai Gorge, Tanzania and was made by Australopithecus Afarensis
