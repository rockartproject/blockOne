Display Description:

This Paris Basin flint scraper with a blond to white patina of the Middle Paleolithic Period of the Mousterian type of debitage in the Levallois method, 100-30 kya. Paris Basin patina is the result of an alteration of flint that transforms the cortex or rind from a white to blond coating by adsorption of calcium carbonates and other minerals (in the soil in an extremely long process that can reach a depth of 0.5 mm. This specimen shows an age toward the beginning of this time horizon.

Discussion:

The (2009) of an open-air site of Ormesson (Seine-et-Marne e France) dating to ca 29,000 cal. BP is related to a bison hunt and illustrates a late Mousterian habitation area. Mousterian remains include a lithic industry, a bone-working area, hearths and a red ochre processing station suggesting the application of these ground nodules to ritualized objects. In total this site provides a view of a series of integrated cognitive capacities of Mousterian Homo erectus during the Middle Paleolithic. This particular Levallois Method-Scraper is expertly flaked and illustrates this point!
