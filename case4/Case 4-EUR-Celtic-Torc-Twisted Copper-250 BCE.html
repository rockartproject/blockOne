Display Description:

A penannular bronze torc or neck-ring with an expending twisted body, and scrolled flat-section finials. These torcs were illustrative of the symbolism of the Celtic serpent that was thought to protect the wearer from harm (Roderick and Davidson 1988). An example of such a torc is found on the famous “dying Gaul” or the “dying Galatian,” a Hellenistic sculpture dated to 230-220 BCE and preserved in a marble Roman copy now in the Capitoline Museums, Rome.
